# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Brain Dump is used by developers and technical project owners who manage local-first backlogs, AI-assisted implementation work, and review workflows. They are usually moving between planning, ticket detail, launch, telemetry, and quality-review screens while trying to keep enough context visible to make a decision quickly.

## Product Purpose

Brain Dump turns a project backlog into an operational workspace for AI coding agents. Success means users can see the state of work, understand the context behind a ticket or epic, launch the right provider, inspect cost and telemetry, and complete review loops without hunting through disconnected tools.

## Brand Personality

Direct, technical, and focused. The interface should feel like a serious workbench: dense enough for repeat use, calm enough for long sessions, and distinctive without becoming decorative.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS gloss, oversized empty layouts, decorative card grids, weird letter spacing, and interface choices that read as AI-generated styling. Do not hide important content inside cramped panels or scroll traps.

## Design Principles

- Put operational context on the canvas instead of burying it in narrow panels.
- Favor predictable product UI patterns over novelty unless the interaction earns it.
- Keep density intentional: compact controls, readable prose, and enough width for long technical content.
- Make failure and overflow states visible; content should never be trapped by layout.
- Use visual personality through proportion, rhythm, and restraint, not decorative effects.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard-operable product controls. Respect reduced-motion preferences, avoid text overflow, and preserve readable line lengths for long prose while allowing data-heavy sections to use wider layouts.
